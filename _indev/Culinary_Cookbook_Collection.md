## 1. Japanese Fried Noodles (Yakisoba)
*Source: scan25.pdf*

### Description
Yakisoba is a classic Japanese stir-fried noodle dish characterized by its savory, slightly sweet, and tangy sauce. It is a popular street food and comfort meal that can be prepared quickly using fresh vegetables and thinly sliced pork.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 10 minutes
* **Total Time:** 25 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 2 servings
* **Course:** Lunch, Entree, Brunch

### Ingredients
* 2 servings pre-steamed noodles for yakisoba
* 1/2 carrot
* 2 cabbage leaves
* 1/4 lb thinly sliced pork
* 1 green pepper
* 1/4 onion
* 4-6 tbsps yakisoba sauce (or yakisoba seasoning powder from a kit)
* Vegetable oil
* Ao-nori (dried green seaweed) for garnish
* Beni-shoga (red pickled ginger) for garnish

### Instructions
1.  **Prepare Ingredients:** Loosen the pre-steamed yakisoba noodles and set them aside. Cut the carrot and green pepper into thin rectangles. Slice the onion. Cut the cabbage leaves and pork into bite-sized pieces.
2.  **Stir-Fry Meat & Aromatics:** Heat some vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the sliced pork and fry until cooked through. 
3.  **Add Hard Vegetables:** Toss in the carrot, onion, and green pepper, stir-frying them until they begin to soften.
4.  **Add Cabbage & Noodles:** Add the cabbage into the wok and stir-fry briefly with the other ingredients. Place the noodles directly on top of the mixture.
5.  **Steam:** Pour 1/4 cup of water over the noodles, then immediately cover the wok with a lid. Turn down the heat to low and let it steam for a few minutes to soften the noodles.
6.  **Sauce & Finish:** Remove the lid and add the yakisoba seasoning powder or sauce (adjusting the amount to your personal preference). Stir-fry vigorously to coat all ingredients evenly.
7.  **Serve:** Transfer to plates, sprinkle with ao-nori, and garnish with a side of beni-shoga.

## 2. French Toast for Raclette Grills (Raclette Déjeuner au Pain Doré)
*Source: scan26.pdf*

### Description
An innovative take on traditional French toast, perfectly tailored for a breakfast or brunch interactive raclette party. Using sliced baguette gives it a delightful texture.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 10 minutes (+30 mins refrigeration)
* **Cook Time:** 5 minutes per batch
* **Total Time:** 45 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 4 servings
* **Course:** Breakfast, Brunch

### Ingredients
* 3 eggs
* 2 to 3 drops of vanilla extract
* 30 mL (2 tbsp) sugar
* 1 pinch of cinnamon
* 375 mL (1 1/2 cups) milk
* 1/2 baguette bread
* Canola oil (for greasing the plate)
* Toppings of your choice (fruit, maple syrup, chocolate, etc.)

### Instructions
1.  **Prep Toppings:** Prepare the breakfast toppings of your choice and set them aside.
2.  **Create Batter:** In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, vanilla extract, sugar, cinnamon, and milk. Cover and reserve in the refrigerator to keep chilled.
3.  **Slice Bread:** Cut the half baguette into even slices about 1.5 cm (1/2 inch) thick.
4.  **Heat Grill:** When ready to eat, preheat your raclette grill. Lightly oil the top cooking plate with canola oil.
5.  **Soak & Cook:** Divide the egg mixture into four separate bowls for guests. Dip the baguette slices into the batter, allowing them to soak for 1 to 2 minutes. Drain excess liquid, then place the slices onto the hot raclette plate. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes on each side until golden brown. Serve hot with your preferred toppings.

## 4. Pineapple Upside-Down Cake (Renversé à l'Ananas)
*Source: scan28.pdf*

### Description
A vintage-style dessert featuring a rich caramelized brown sugar topping decorated with juicy pineapple rings and maraschino cherries, baked under a simple, soft sponge cake.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 45 minutes
* **Total Time:** 1 hour
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate
* **Yield:** 6-8 servings
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
**For the Batter:**
* 1 1/3 cups flour
* 3/4 cup sugar
* 3 tsps baking powder
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1/4 cup Crisco (shortening)
* 1 egg, beaten
* 3/4 cup milk

**For the Caramel Topping:**
* 3 tbsps butter
* 1/2 cup brown sugar (cassonade)
* 6 pineapple slices
* 6 maraschino cherries

### Instructions
1.  **Preheat & Base:** Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Melt 3 tablespoons of butter directly in your baking pan.
2.  **Make Caramel:** Sprinkle the brown sugar evenly over the melted butter. Heat gently until it browns and forms a uniform caramel layer, then remove from heat. Arrange the 6 pineapple slices beautifully on top of the caramel, placing a maraschino cherry in the center of each ring.
3.  **Mix Dry Ingredients:** In a mixing bowl, combine the un-sifted flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
4.  **Cut Fat:** Cut the shortening into the dry ingredients using a pastry cutter or forks until the mixture resembles small, pea-sized crumbs. Create a well in the center.
5.  **Add Liquids:** Pour the beaten egg and milk into the well. Mix just enough until the batter becomes smooth and homogeneous (do not overmix).
6.  **Bake:** Spread the cake batter smoothly over the arranged pineapple and cherries. Bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes. 
7.  **Invert:** Let cool for a few minutes, then carefully invert the cake onto a serving platter so the pineapple arrangement faces up.

## 5. Rice Krispies Squares (Carrés aux Rice Krispies)
*Source: scan29.pdf*

### Description
The ultimate classic, crispy, marshmallow treat loved by families everywhere. Quick to make on either the stovetop or via microwave.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 10 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 5 minutes
* **Total Time:** 15 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Very Easy
* **Yield:** 24 bars (5 cm / 2")
* **Course:** Snack / Treat

### Ingredients
* 1/4 cup margarine or butter
* 40 regular marshmallows (or 5 cups miniature marshmallows)
* 6 cups Kellogg's Rice Krispies cereal
* 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
* *(Tip: For chewier squares, add an extra 1 cup of marshmallows / approx. 8 regular marshmallows)*

### Instructions
#### Stovetop Method
1.  **Melt:** Melt the margarine or butter in a large saucepan over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir continuously until completely melted and well blended. Remove from heat immediately.
2.  **Coat:** Stir in the vanilla extract if using. Add the Rice Krispies cereal and mix gently until every grain is thoroughly coated with marshmallow.
3.  **Press & Cool:** Using a lightly buttered spatula or wax paper, press the mixture firmly and evenly into a greased 13" x 9" pan. Allow to cool completely before cutting into squares.

#### Microwave Method
1.  Microwave butter/margarine in a large microwave-safe bowl on HIGH (100%) for 30-60 seconds until melted.
2.  Add marshmallows, tossing to coat. Microwave on HIGH for 1.5 minutes or until smooth when stirred, making sure to stir halfway through (at 45 seconds).
3.  Follow steps 2 and 3 from the stovetop method.

## 6. Classic Apple Crisp (Croustade aux Pommes)
*Source: scan30.pdf*

### Description
A warm, traditional French-Canadian style fruit crisp packed with sweet spiced apples and topped with a generous, buttery brown sugar oat crumble.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 10 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 30 minutes
* **Total Time:** 40 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 8 portions
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
**Apple Mixture:**
* 8 large apples, peeled and cored
* 30 mL (2 tbsps) water
* 30 mL (2 tbsps) lemon juice
* 125 mL (1/2 cup) white sugar
* 2 mL (1/2 tsp) cinnamon

**Oat Crumble Topping:**
* 150 mL (2/3 cup) flour
* 500 mL (2 cups) oats / gruau
* 150 mL (2/3 cup) brown sugar (cassonade)
* 150 mL (2/3 cup) margarine or butter
* 1 mL (1/4 tsp) cinnamon

### Instructions
1.  **Prep Apples:** Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). Cut the cored and peeled apples into small bite-sized pieces and spread them evenly across a baking dish.
2.  **Season Apples:** Add the water, lemon juice, white sugar, and 2 mL of cinnamon directly to the baking dish. Stir thoroughly to coat the apples.
3.  **Make Topping:** In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, margarine, and 1 mL of cinnamon. Work the mixture together until a crumbly dough forms.
4.  **Assemble & Bake:** Evenly spread the oat crumble over the apple layer. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes until the apples are bubbling and the oat topping is deeply golden brown.

## 7. The Ultimate Maple Syrup Pie (Tarte au Sirop d'Érable - La Meilleure)
*Source: scan31.pdf*

### Description
Authored by Ricardo, this is a premium version of Canada's traditional maple pie. It uses heavy cream and a touch of cornstarch to get a perfectly smooth, rich custard-like filling inside a scratch-made flaky crust.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 25 minutes
* **Chill/Wait Time:** 2 hours 30 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 45 minutes
* **Total Time:** 3 hours 40 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate
* **Yield:** 8 servings
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
**Flaky Crust:**
* 190 g (1 1/4 cups) all-purpose unbleached flour
* 2.5 mL (1/2 tsp) baking powder
* 1 mL (1/4 tsp) salt
* 115 g (1/2 cup) cold unsalted butter, cubed
* 45 mL (3 tbsps) ice-cold water

**Rich Maple Filling:**
* 30 mL (2 tbsps) cornstarch
* 30 mL (2 tbsps) all-purpose unbleached flour
* 375 mL (1 1/2 cups) amber maple syrup
* 125 mL (1/2 cup) heavy cream (35%)
* 1 egg

### Instructions
1.  **Form Dough:** In a food processor, pulse the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and pulse a few seconds at a time until the butter pieces are the size of small peas. Add the ice water and blend until the dough just begins to come together. Shape into a smooth disc.
2.  **Roll & Chill:** On a lightly farined work surface, roll out the pastry dough to a thickness of about 3 mm (1/8 inch). Press into a 23 cm (9-inch) glass pie pan. Trim any excess edges. Place in the refrigerator to chill for 30 minutes.
3.  **Preheat Oven:** Move your oven rack to the lowest position. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
4.  **Whisk Filling:** In a mixing bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and flour. Slowly pour in the maple syrup, whisking constantly until perfectly smooth and the starch is fully dissolved. Whisk in the heavy cream and the egg.
5.  **Bake:** Pour the smooth maple filling into the chilled pie shell. Bake for 45 minutes until the filling is set and the crust is beautifully golden. Allow to cool completely and rest for at least 2 hours before slicing.

## 8. Strawberry Rhubarb Lattice Pie (Tarte à la Rhubarbe et aux Fraises)
*Source: scan32.pdf*

### Description
A gorgeous summer dessert pairing tart rhubarb with sweet strawberries beneath a beautiful woven pastry lattice.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 30 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 45 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Advanced
* **Yield:** 8 servings
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
* 4 cups (1 L) chopped rhubarb
* 2 cups (500 mL) fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
* 1 1/3 cups (330 mL) sugar
* 1/4 cup (60 mL) cornstarch
* 1 tbsp (15 mL) lemon juice
* 1/4 tsp (1 mL) ground cinnamon
* Prepared pastry dough for a double-crust 9-inch (23 cm) pie
* 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

### Instructions
1.  **Mix Fruit Filling:** In a large bowl, mix together the chopped rhubarb, sliced strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and cinnamon until evenly combined.
2.  **Roll Bottom Crust:** Roll out half of your pastry dough into a 10-inch (25 cm) circle. Place it inside a 9-inch pie dish. Spoon the fruit filling into the crust. Brush the outer rim of the dough with some beaten egg.
3.  **Weave Lattice:** Roll out the remaining half of the dough into a 9-inch circle. Cut into strips about 1 inch (2.5 cm) wide using a knife or pastry wheel. Arrange strips over the fruit filling in an alternating lattice pattern. Press the edges down to seal, trim any excess, and crimp the border beautifully. Brush the entire top lattice with the remaining beaten egg.
4.  **Bake:** Place the pie pan onto a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for 15 minutes. Then, turn down the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue baking for 50 to 60 minutes more. The pie is ready when the rhubarb is perfectly tender, the filling is bubbling thick, and the crust is golden brown.

## 9. Decadent Chocolate Mousse (Mousse au Chocolat)
*Source: scan33.pdf*

### Description
An ultra-rich, silky French chocolate mousse lightened up with perfectly whipped egg whites and cream.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 20 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 5 minutes
* **Total Time:** 25 minutes (+ chilling time)
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate
* **Yield:** 6 servings

### Ingredients
* 1 whole bar of Baker's sweet chocolate (or 1 1/3 cups of chocolate chips)
* A small splash of water
* 1 knob of butter
* 7 egg yolks
* 7 egg whites
* 500 mL (1 demiard) heavy cream
* 2 tbsps powdered sugar

### Instructions
1.  **Melt Chocolate:** Melt the chocolate bar or chips with a splash of water in a saucepan over low heat. Stir in a piece of butter until glossy, then remove from the heat source.
2.  **Tempering Yolks:** Add the 7 egg yolks one by one into the chocolate mixture, mixing vigorously after each addition. Allow the mixture to cool down.
3.  **Whip Cream:** In a chilled bowl, whip the 500 mL of heavy cream along with 2 tablespoons of powdered sugar until stiff peaks form. Gently fold this whipped cream into the cooled chocolate mixture.
4.  **Whip Whites & Fold:** In a separate clean bowl, beat the 7 egg whites until stiff peaks form. Carefully fold your chocolate-cream mixture into the whipped egg whites until completely homogeneous. Pour into serving cups and chill thoroughly before serving.

### Description
A nostalgic, no-bake layered refrigerator cake that softens into a beautiful cake-like texture overnight.

## 11. Alternative Skillet Apple Crisp (Croustillant aux Pommes)
*Source: scan33.pdf*

### Description
A rustic apple crisp variety that relies on finely diced butter worked directly into flour or oats for a crispy crumb layer.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 1 hour
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 6 servings

### Ingredients
**Base:**
* 4 cups sliced apples
* 1/3 cup sugar
* 2 tbsps flour
* 1/2 tsp cinnamon
* 1 tbsp butter or margarine, diced
* 2 tbsps lemon juice

**Topping:**
* 3/4 cup brown sugar
* 3/4 cup sifted flour (or oats)

## 13. Bulk Pastry Dough (Pâte à Tarte)
*Source: scan33.pdf*

### Description
The ultimate secret to a perfect pastry crust: keeping all ingredients freezing cold. This recipe makes enough dough for multiple pies.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Resting Time:** 1 hour
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate

### Ingredients
* 5 cups flour
* 1 lb shortening
* 1 cup ice water
* 1 egg, beaten

### Instructions
1.  **Chill Equipment:** Place all ingredients and kitchen utensils into the refrigerator beforehand; this is the key to perfect pastry dough.
2.  **Cut Fat:** Once completely chilled, work the shortening into the flour by cutting it into cubes and combining until crumbly. Pour in the ice-cold water and beaten egg.
3.  **Form:** Mix together rapidly using your hands. Form into discs and store in the refrigerator for 1 hour to rest. Roll out and bake immediately to ensure the shortening doesn't melt before hitting the oven.

## 15. Gourmet Spice Cake (Gâteau aux Épices Gourmet)
*Source: scan34.pdf*

### Description
A deeply fragrant, old-fashioned molasses cake bursting with warm autumn spices like ginger, nutmeg, and cinnamon.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 35-60 minutes (depending on pan type)
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate
* **Yield:** 12-16 servings

### Ingredients
* 2 1/2 cups flour
* 2 tsps baking powder
* 1 tsp shortening
* 1 cup brown sugar
* 1 cup molasses
* 1 tsp ground ginger
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp nutmeg
* 1/2 tsp cinnamon
* 1 cup boiling water
* 1 tsp baking soda (soda à pâte)
* 2 eggs, well beaten

### Instructions
1.  **Dry Mix:** Mix together the flour, baking powder, and ground ginger in a bowl.
2.  **Cream Fat & Aromatics:** In a separate large bowl, cream together the shortening and brown sugar. Thoroughly blend in the molasses, salt, nutmeg, and cinnamon.
3.  **Combine:** Pour the boiling water over the baking soda, then add this mixture directly to your creamed molasses base. Gradually add the wet mixture into the dry ingredients, stirring well until smooth. Fold in the beaten eggs and blend uniformly.
4.  **Bake:** Pour into a greased and floured 9" x 13" x 2" rectangular baking pan, or into two 8" square baking pans. Bake at 325°F (165°C). 
    * *Rectangular pan:* Bake for 50 to 60 minutes.
    * *Square pans:* Bake for 35 to 40 minutes.
5.  **Serve:** Enjoy warm or cold with whipped cream or a layer of cream cheese frosting.

## 16. Applesauce Raisin Cake (Gâteau Compote de Pommes)
*Source: scan34.pdf*

### Description
An incredibly moist and dense fruit cake featuring warm applesauce, cloves, and flour-dusted Sultana and currant raisins.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 20 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 1 hour
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 20 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate

### Ingredients
* 1 3/4 cups sifted flour
* 1 tsp baking soda
* 1/4 tsp salt
* 1 tsp cinnamon
* 1/2 tsp ground cloves
* 1/2 cup butter, margarine, or liquid Crisco
* 1 cup sugar
* 1 egg, white beaten to stiff peaks (keep yolk separate)
* 1 cup Sultana raisins, lightly dusted in flour
* 1 cup Currant raisins (raisins de Corinthe)
* 1 cup thick, warm applesauce

### Instructions
1.  **Sift Dry Ingredients:** Sift the flour once. Add the salt, baking soda, cinnamon, and ground cloves. Sift the whole mixture together 3 more times to aerate.
2.  **Cream Base:** Cream the butter/fat with the sugar. Mix in the single egg yolk until light and fluffy.
3.  **Fold & Layer:** Gently blend in the flour mixture along with the stiff-peaked egg white. Stir in the warm, thick applesauce and fold in the flour-dusted Sultana and currant raisins. The resulting batter will be very thick. Whip vigorously to smooth it out.
4.  **Bake:** Pour into a prepared pan and bake for approximately 1 hour at 325°F to 350°F (165°C - 175°C).

## 18. Poor Man's Pudding (Pouding Chômeur)
*Source: scan35.pdf*

### Description
A legendary French-Canadian comfort food dessert created during the Great Depression. It features a simple cake batter baked directly beneath a boiling brown sugar syrup, which sinks to the bottom during baking to create a rich, sweet caramel sauce base.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 1 hour
* **Total Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 6-8 servings
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
**For the Syrup:**
* 1 3/4 cups brown sugar (*cassonade*)
* 1 3/4 cups water

**For the Cake Batter:**
* 1 1/3 cups flour (*1 tasse 1/3*)
* 3/4 cup white sugar
* 3 tsps baking powder (*poudre à pâte*)
* 1/4 tsp salt
* 1/4 cup shortening (*graisse*)
* 1 egg, beaten
* 3/4 cup milk

### Instructions
1. **Prepare Syrup:** In a saucepan, combine the brown sugar and water. Bring to a boil and let boil for 10 minutes. Remove from heat and allow to cool down to lukewarm.
2. **Combine Dry Batter:** In a mixing bowl, mix the flour, white sugar, baking powder, and salt together.
3. **Cut Fat:** Cut the shortening into the dry ingredients until it forms small, pea-sized crumbs.
4. **Mix Liquids:** Pour in the beaten egg and milk, and mix until the batter is homogeneous. Transfer the batter into a greased baking pan.
5. **Assemble & Bake:** Pour the lukewarm syrup directly over the raw cake batter. Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (180°C) for 1 hour.

## 19. Frozen Strawberry Mousse (Mousse Glacée aux Fraises)
*Source: scan36.pdf*

### Description
A light, airy, and refreshing summer dessert combining a sweet strawberry purée, fluffy whipped egg whites, and rich cream.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 20 minutes
* **Freezing Time:** 2 hours
* **Total Time:** 2 hours 20 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Intermediate
* **Yield:** 6-8 servings
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
* 2 egg whites
* 1 pinch of salt
* 1/4 cup fruit sugar (*sucre à fruits*)
* 3 cups fresh strawberries
* 1/2 tsp (2 mL) almond extract
* 1/2 cup heavy cream (35%)
* Extra whole strawberries coated in fruit sugar (for garnish)

### Instructions
1. **Purée Strawberries:** Take the 3 cups of fresh strawberries and process them into a smooth purée. Set aside.
2. **Beat Egg Whites:** In a large bowl, beat the 2 egg whites with a pinch of salt until frothy and bubbly.
3. **Whip the Meringue:** Gradually add the strawberry purée, almond extract, and 1/4 cup of fruit sugar into the egg whites. Beat on high speed with an electric mixer until the mixture forms stiff peaks.
4. **Fold Cream:** In a separate bowl, whip the 35% heavy cream until stiff. Gently fold the whipped cream into the strawberry-egg white mixture (do not overmix or stir heavily).
5. **Freeze:** Place the mixture into the freezer for approximately 2 hours until thoroughly chilled and set.
6. **Serve:** Let it rest at room temperature for 10 minutes before serving. Scoop into dessert glasses and garnish with whole fruit-sugar-coated strawberries.

## 25. Food Processor Pie Crust (Pâte à Tarte au Robot)
*Source: scan42.pdf*

### Description
A modern, foolproof method to create four incredibly flaky pie crusts using a food processor to rapidly cut ice-cold butter into flour.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 10 minutes
* **Chilling Time:** 30 minutes
* **Total Time:** 40 minutes
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 4 pie crusts (*4 croûtes de tarte*)

### Ingredients
* 3 cups flour
* 1 tsp salt
* 1 cup cold butter, cut into small cubes
* 1 egg yolk
* Ice-cold water

### Instructions
1. **Pulse Dry Ingredients:** Put the flour and salt into the bowl of your food processor. Pulse a few times until mixed. 
2. **Add Fat:** Add the cold cubed butter into the processor and pulse until the texture resembles coarse breadcrumbs.
3. **Measure Liquids:** In a liquid measuring cup, beat the egg yolk, then add enough ice water until the volume reaches exactly 2/3 cup. 
4. **Form Dough:** Gradually stream the liquid mixture into the running food processor little by little until a cohesive dough ball begins to form. (If it is too dry, add extra water 1 tablespoon at a time).
5. **Portion and Chill:** Divide the large dough ball into 4 equal discs. Wrap each disc tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before rolling out.

## 26. Classic Angel Food Strawberry Shortcake (Shortcake aux Fraises)
*Source: scan43.pdf*

### Description
A showstopping, light, and airy summer dessert featuring a three-tier homemade vanilla Angel Food cake layered with heaps of sweetened macerated strawberries and thick vanilla whipped cream.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 40 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 20 minutes
* **Resting Time:** 1 hour
* **Total Time:** 2 hours
* **Difficulty:** Advanced
* **Yield:** 10 portions
* **Course:** Dessert

### Ingredients
**For the Angel Food Cake Base:**
* 10 egg whites
* 1 pinch of salt
* 2.5 mL (1 1/2 tsps) cream of tartar
* 250 mL (1 cup) granulated sugar
* 5 mL (1 tsp) vanilla extract
* 310 mL (1 1/4 cups) all-purpose flour

**For the Sweetened Strawberries:**
* 900 g (2 lbs) fresh strawberries, diced
* 45 mL (3 tbsps) sugar
* Extra whole strawberries for top decoration

**For the Vanilla Whipped Cream:**
* 500 mL (2 cups) 35% heavy whipping cream
* 250 mL (1 cup) powdered sugar (*sucre à glacer*)
* 5 mL (1 tsp) vanilla extract

### Instructions
1. **Preheat:** Preheat your oven to 163°C / 325°F.
2. **Whip Egg Whites:** Using an electric mixer, whip the 10 egg whites with a pinch of salt until soft peaks form. Ajouter la crème de tartre, le sucre et la vanille en fouettant.
3. **Fold Flour:** In a second bowl, sift the flour. Gradually and carefully fold the sifted flour into the whipped meringue using a spatula until just integrated (do not deflate the mixture).
4. **Bake:** Lightly spray three 9-inch (23 cm) round baking pans with cooking spray. Evenly distribute the delicate batter among the three pans. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes until the cake tops are lightly golden brown.
5. **Invert and Cool:** Immediately remove cakes from the oven, flip the pans upside down onto wire cooling racks, and let them cool/rest for 1 hour.
6. **Macerate Berries:** While the cake cools, mix the 900g of diced strawberries with 3 tablespoons of sugar in a bowl and set aside to let juices release.
7. **Whip Topping:** In a clean bowl, beat the heavy cream until soft peaks form, then add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, whipping until stiff peaks form.
8. **Assemble Layer Cake:** Carefully run a knife along the inner edges of the cake pans to release the sponge layers. Lay down the first cake tier on your serving plate. Spread a thick layer of whipped cream followed by a generous spoonful of sweet strawberries. Stack the second cake tier on top and repeat the cream and berry layer. Crown with the final third cake layer, spread the remaining whipped cream over the top, and decorate elegantly with whole strawberries.

## 27. Apple Oat Crumble Bars (Carré aux Pommes)
*Source: scan44.pdf*

### Description
A golden, granular oat pastry bar that sandwiches a thick, spiced apple filling between two crisp oat and brown sugar crumble layers.

### At a Glance
* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook Time:** 45 minutes
* **Total Time:** 1 hour
* **Difficulty:** Easy
* **Yield:** 9 square bars
* **Course:** Dessert / Snack

### Ingredients
* 1 cup oats (*gruau*)
* 3/4 cup flour
* 1/2 tsp baking soda (*soda*)
* 3/4 cup brown sugar (*cassonade*)
* 3/4 cup vegetable shortening (*graisse végétale*)
* Prepared apple filling layer (separate recipe)

### Instructions
1. **Prep Pan:** Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease an 8" x 8" square baking pan.
2. **Make Crumble Mix:** In a large bowl, mix together the oats, flour, baking soda, and brown sugar.
3. **Work Fat:** Add the vegetable shortening into the dry ingredients. Work the mixture together using a fork first, and then rub it together with your fingers until the dough is entirely crumbly and granular.
4. **Layer Base:** Press exactly half of this crumble mixture firmly into the bottom of your greased square pan.
5. **Fruit & Top Layer:** Cover the base evenly with your prepared apple pie filling mixture. Scatter the remaining half of the oat crumble dough over the top of the apples, and tap down lightly to level it.
6. **Bake:** Bake for 45 minutes at 350°F until the apples are tender and bubbling, and the top oat crust is beautifully golden brown.
